Html5 video element

To show a video on your page you need to use the video element.

The video element comes with a few attributes that allow the video to be paused, played, and volume control.

You must include the source element within the video element. The source element allows you to have alternative video formats.

Supported video formats:

Chrome, opera 25, and Firefox support MP4, WebM, and ogg.

Safari and internet explorer only support MP4 video formats.

HTML5 defines the DOM properties, methods, and events for the video. This allows for the use of JavaScrirpt to manipulate audio and video elements.

Html5 video in action

When embedding a video you have the option to show controls.

You can also use the poster attribute to have an image show up instead until the video plays.

If the video you want to play has sound you don't want playing, you can mute the video with the mute attribute.

There is also an autoplay attrubute that plays the video when it is ready.
